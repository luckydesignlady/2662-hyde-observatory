Hyde Memorial Observatory

Your Window on the Universe

Hyde Memorial Observatory is open to public viewing, completely free of admission charge, and currently open every Saturday Night from 7 to 10pm. It is fully operated by volunteers, we feature views of the night skies through our three telescopes and hold a public astronomy presentation every Saturday night.

Hyde Memorial Observatory is, as far as we know, the only observatory anywhere that is: built, furnished and operated entirely by donations, devoted exclusively to public viewing, completely free of admission charge, and staffed only by volunteers.

Hours & Location

Hyde is open every Saturday night year round. We may be closed if Saturday falls on or very near a major holiday. If in doubt, call first.

Hyde Memorial Observatory is located on the south side of Holmes Lake in Southeast Lincoln Nebraska at 3701 South 70th Street.

January Program #1: 'POINT OF NO RETURN'

Describes the exotic science of quasars and super massive black holes. Running time: 20 minutes.

January Program #2: 'THE WINTER CONSTELLATIONS'

A tour of the evening sky above Lincoln, Nebraska diring the frigid winter months: Perfect viewing weather when it's not snowing. Running time: 20 minutes.
